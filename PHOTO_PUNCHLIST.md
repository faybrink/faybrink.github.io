# Fay Brink Site — Photography Punchlist
*For area guides, blog posts, and magazine-style editorial layout*

---

## How to Use This List

**Shot types needed per page:**
- **Hero** — wide/cinematic (shoot landscape, 16:9 or wider). Goes in the page header.
- **Community** — square-ish, detail-rich. Goes in the neighborhood card grid.
- **Editorial** — anything that tells a story in one frame. Goes inline in blog posts.
- **Portrait** — vertical orientation for mobile-forward card layouts.

**Time of day matters:** Golden hour (45 min after sunrise, 45 min before sunset) for exteriors. Overcast days actually work well for real estate — no harsh shadows. Avoid midday sun.

**AI substitute?** Marked where AI-generated imagery is a reasonable stand-in. AI works well for: generic interiors, concept/lifestyle, weather/mood. AI does NOT work well for: specific Houston landmarks, recognizable streets, anything that needs to look like a real place people will recognize.

## AI IMAGE PRIORITIES

**Best candidates for AI generation (use Midjourney or DALL-E 3):**
1. Generic Houston suburban streetscapes (without specific landmarks)
2. Real estate closing/contract signing scenes
3. Inspector with clipboard in new construction framing
4. Stylized "lifestyle" shots — coffee at a desk, family walking a trail
5. Abstract editorial images for blog headers — flood conceptual, school books, compass/map imagery

**Do NOT use AI for:**
- Anything you want to look like a real Houston location
- Any shot where viewers might recognize the place
- Headshots or any image with faces
- Anything going in Google Business Profile or MLS-adjacent contexts
